🌱 Day one: Butchart Gardens

Brittany has one day — gates open 8 AM, close 6 PM — to photograph the famous sights of a 55-acre garden built inside an old limestone quarry.

Time only passes when Brittany acts. Each step costs ~1⅓ minutes (grass costs more), and every press of the shutter costs 4 minutes — even the botched ones. Standing still is free: think, study the sundial, plan. In shade, she can also Rest (🌳 button or R) — 10 minutes pass and sun exposure heals at double speed, no water spent. In open sun, there is no resting: the shade won't come to her.

Photograph at least 4 of the 10 targets, keep her hydrated (💧), stay out of the sun (☀️ shade heals as she travels through it), and reach a gate before her time runs out.

Brittany's husband Matt is also somewhere in the garden — wandering, chatting with strangers, admiring the roses, and orbiting his wife. He is no help whatsoever, he sometimes wanders absent-mindedly into her shots, but he loves her very much.

Move: arrow keys / WASD / d-pad  ·  Photograph: stand beside a 📷 marker, press Space or the button
Leaving: walk up to a gate (east edge, where Brittany starts) and press the 🚪 button that appears.
Shade moves with the sun. Drag the sundial scrubber to preview where shadows will be later.
Water fountains (blue ⛪) refill hydration — but each works only once, then runs dry. Four fountains = four drinks. Spend them wisely. Beware children near famous landmarks.
Once per day, while the camera is raised, Brittany can 🦝 Spirit Cast a raccoon (C) to clear the frame; it then keeps watch from just outside the shot. ("Spirit cast" is a euphemism. It is a real raccoon.)
